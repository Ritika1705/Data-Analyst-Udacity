SUMMARY AND CONCLUSION¶

The loan dataset has a large number columns from which the following conclusions can be drawn:

The loan can be issued for a period of 36,12 or 60 months. The people issuing loan for a period of 36 months is the greatest followed by 60 months and 12 months.

In the loan status column, the people with "current" loan status are the maximum followed by "completed".

In the CreditGrade category, Category 'C' has the highest number of loan issuers folllowed by 'B'.

In the borrowerRate, we plot a histogram or a distance graph, from which we can clearly see that the average BorrowerRate is around 2 percent.

Next the scatterplot of Lenderyield is plotted against BorrowerRate and BorrowerAPR from which we can conclude that, Borrower APR is a better measure of calculations.

Violin plots of LoanStatus vs LenderYield gives us a clear picture.

When we plot the graph of two categorical variables i.e Term and Grade, we can see almost everyone took a loan for a period of 36 months.

Similarly, term vs loanstatus was also plotted.

From the Loan dataset we can find that maximum people issuing loans have an income range between 25,000-49,999 $
